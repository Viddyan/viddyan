BCI SHUTS DOWN Lawyer Social Media Ads with Celebrity Ban!

Published on November 2, 2024

BCI Takes a Stand Against Lawyer Advertising

The Bar Council of India, or BCI, has made a strong statement. They do not want lawyers to use social media to get clients. This includes making short videos to promote themselves. It also means they cannot pay famous people or online stars to advertise for them. They announced this with a warning on March 17.

Why the BCI is Concerned

The BCI is upset that lawyers are using movie actors and other well-known figures to get attention. They also do not like that lawyers are using the internet to advertise. The BCI says this breaks a rule called Rule 36. This rule says lawyers should not try to get work by sending out ads or talking to people directly. They also should not post photos of themselves with cases.

Lawyering is About Trust, Not Sales

The BCI says that being a lawyer is about trust and doing what is right. It is not like running a business to make money. The Supreme Court of India agrees. They think law is about fairness and honesty, not selling something. The BCI worries that if lawyers act like salespeople, people will not trust them. It will also hurt the reputation of lawyers.

The Incident that Sparked the Change

This announcement comes after some things happened recently. For example, a law company called DSK Legal put a video of the actor Rahul Bose on Instagram. They wanted to show they were the best in the business. Because of things like this, the BCI has made new rules to stop lawyers from advertising in a wrong way.

The New Rules for Lawyers

The rules are: Any ads that break Rule 36 must be taken down right away. Lawyers cannot use actors or online stars to advertise. They cannot put up banners or ads online to get legal work. The BCI will also stop people who are not real lawyers from giving wrong legal advice. Lawyers cannot ask for clients on social media. Also, social media companies should check for ads that are not honest.

Consequences for Breaking the Rules

The BCI warns that lawyers who break these rules will be punished. They could be kicked out of the BCI. They could also be taken to court. The BCI is asking all lawyers and social media sites to follow these rules. This is to keep the lawyer profession honest.

BCI's Previous Warnings

The BCI has talked about this before. In July 2024, after a court decision, they said again that they do not want lawyers advertising in a bad way. The court said being a lawyer is about helping people, not making money. They said online ads make things look bad. The BCI told other lawyer groups to punish lawyers who advertise on certain websites. The court said these websites break the rules for lawyers and the law.

Supreme Court Involvement

The BCI also mentioned a Supreme Court case from 2018. They said they have power over anyone who practices law, no matter what they are called. They also said it is wrong for lawyers to use religious events or public events to put up ads for themselves. This breaks the rules of being a good lawyer.
